Dear Urban Alliance on Race Relations,

I am thrilled to apply for this role, supporting the Urban Alliance on Race Relations in building a website for the Anti-Racism in Education Project. This project excites me not only as a growing web developer and designer but also as a community organizer, anti-racism, equity and accessibility consultant, and journalism graduate who often investigated systemic inequity in education (such as the impacts of academic streaming on Black and brown students).

While spearheading communications and project management for over eight years, I have led with my passion for community building and making information and education more accessible, understandable and engaging to the public. As a J-School graduate and communications professional with experience in non-profits, academia and public service, I have produced high-quality multimedia deliverables for a diversity of stakeholders and audiences, regularly developed and managed e-newsletters, social media channels, websites and web copy, planned and advised on content development, policies and strategies and ensured compliance with brand and content style guidelines and accessibility standards.

Recently, I completed Seneca’s Web Development and Design Certificate Program, deepening my project management, UX design, strategic communications, stakeholder engagement and quality assurance skills when building accessible, cross-browser compatible and responsive websites, as you will see in my portfolio below.

In both my academic and professional career, equity, anti-racism, and accessibility in user experience have been central to my work. Witnessing and experiencing the deeply rooted systemic racism in policies, practices and the very fabric and culture of higher education, journalism schools, workplaces and the field, I worked to establish an Anti-Racism and Equity Committee at Carleton University’s J-School, consulted on curriculum, policies and pedagogical approaches, and organized events and facilitated discussions centring the experiences of Black, Indigenous and people of the global majority, addressing and disrupting how systemic racism and inequity were normalized.

My lived experience, academic and professional background, and commitment to ongoing learning give me the expertise and drive to support the Urban Alliance on Race Relations in designing this new website.

Thank you for taking the time to consider my application. I look forward to hearing from you and discussing this opportunity further.

Sincerely,
Gowlene Selvavijayan

About Me

Gowlene Selvavijayan (they/she) is a Tamil neuroqueer web designer, community organizer, digital communications specialist and storyteller. Born and raised in Scarborough, they moved to Ottawa in 2015 to study journalism and human rights at Carleton University. As a J-School graduate and communications professional of over eight years, they are no stranger to working with trauma-informed, cultural humility and a keen editorial eye while managing multiple overlapping priorities in high-pressure environments with quick turnarounds.

Gowlene is committed to the ongoing learning, practicing and embedding of anti-racism, equity and accessibility in web design and development, communications, community programming and internal policies and practices. In their work within academia, non-profits and the public service and as a freelancer, they have worked to strengthen the impact of research, academic scholarship, and community-based knowledge and make data, information and stories more accessible, compelling, practical, and understandable. Their work and strategic advice have supported organizations in fostering safety, community, knowledge sharing, and greater access to resources, especially for racialized, disabled, neurodivergent, queer, trans and gender diverse youth.

Gowlene takes pride in their curiosity, creativity, and ability to quickly learn new skills and technology to see a project to its utmost potential, foster empowered collaboration, streamline internal processes, and support organizational growth and transformation.
